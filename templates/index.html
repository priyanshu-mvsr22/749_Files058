History Of Phishing.

  • 1990s

    Origin and Early Methods

    Phishing emerged alongside the rise of the internet. Early attacks often targeted AOL users through social engineering tactics, where attackers impersonated AOL staff to steal login credentials.

  • 2000s

    Rapid Growth with Email Phishing

    Phishing shifted to emails, with attackers sending fake messages mimicking banks, e-commerce sites, or online services. The first major phishing attack occurred in 2004, targeting users of Citibank, and marked the rise of large-scale credential theft.

  • 2010s

    Sophistication and Targeted Attacks

    Phishing attacks became more advanced with spear-phishing (customized attacks on individuals) and whaling (targeting executives). Attackers exploited social media and cloud platforms. High-profile , such as the 2016 DNC email hack, highlighted the dangers of phishing.

  • July 2020

    Automation and Evolving Tactics

    Modern phishing leverages AI and automation to enhance scalability and deception, such as deepfake attacks and fake websites. COVID-19 scams targeting health and financial information also surged, reflecting the adaptation of phishing to current events.
